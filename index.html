Berkane City

Berkane is a city in northeastern Morocco, in the tribal area of Aït Iznasen, limited by the Mediterranean to the north, the Kis river and Oujda Province in the east, Nador Province to the west, and Taourirt Province in the south. It is the capital of Berkane Province.

Located in northeastern Morocco, in the Rif region. It is the capital of the Berkane Province. The city is situated about 60 kilometers south of the Mediterranean Sea, near the border with Algeria. Berkane is known as the "Citrus Capital" of Morocco because of its vast citrus groves and is a major producer of oranges, clementines, and other citrus fruits. The city has a Mediterranean climate, characterized by hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters. The economy of Berkane is primarily based on agriculture, with citrus fruits being the major export product. However, it is also a commercial and administrative center for the surrounding region. Berkane is also a gateway to the nearby Rif Mountains, which are a popular destination for hikers and nature enthusiasts. The city itself has a mix of modern and traditional architecture, with a lively central market and several mosques. The population is predominantly Arab and Berber, and the primary languages spoken are Moroccan Arabic and Berber, although French is also widely understood and used in business and government. Berkane is not only a hub for agriculture but also a city rich in culture and history. The region is home to several annual festivals, and the lively central market is a must-visit for anyone looking to experience the local culture and cuisine. The surrounding region offers a variety of outdoor activities, from hiking in the Rif Mountains to relaxing on the Mediterranean beaches. Whether you are a resident or a visitor, Berkane has something to offer for everyone.
